How does the watermark work?

Metadata is hidden inside WAV files using LSB (Least Significant Bit) steganography. Each character of your message is encoded into the lowest bit of consecutive 16-bit audio samples. The change is imperceptible to the human ear — each sample shifts by at most ±1 on a 65,536-level scale (>80 dB SNR). The embedded data includes a magic header and checksum for reliable extraction. One second of audio can hold ~5.5 KB of hidden text. To extract a watermark, drop a WAV file in the section below.
